Why not pure black & pure white?
Off-white page background
Pure white (#FFFFFF) on a pure white page makes cards invisible — there's nothing to distinguish surfaces. A warm off-white (#F8F8F6) gives the page depth while keeping the "clean" feel. Cards sit on top in true white, creating a natural hierarchy without shadows.
Charcoal instead of pure black
Pure black (#000000) text on white creates harsh contrast that feels cheap and tiring to read. A near-black charcoal (#1A1A1A for headlines, #2C2C2A for body) reads as luxurious and editorial — it's how premium brands like Linear, Notion, and Apple style their type.
Why blue works as the accent
Your instinct on blue is correct. Blue (#378ADD) is the strongest choice for DocuSmart because it signals trust, professionalism, and technology — exactly what a career platform needs. It also has the best contrast ratio on white for CTAs and passes WCAG AA accessibility on all text sizes.
Why not purple as tertiary?
Purple would work but it reads more "creative/playful" — great for a design tool, slightly off for a career platform targeting Cameroonian employers and scholarship committees. Blue keeps DocuSmart in the same visual language as LinkedIn, Workday, and global career platforms users already trust.
